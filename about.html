Origin of the rhythm

Founded by Lukeman Kyotoyinze

Rwambera Drumline was born in the heart of Busabala, a lakeside community where drumming is the language of joy. What started as informal gatherings under mango trees has grown into a celebrated cultural ensemble.

In 2012, master drummer Lukeman Kyotoyinze gathered young talents from surrounding villages, passing down rhythms that have echoed for generations. Today, the drumline carries that legacy—each beat connecting past and present, village and world.

The name "Rwambera" comes from a local Luganda phrase meaning "the ones who awaken" – a tribute to the drums that call communities together at dawn for celebrations and ceremonies.

Early rehearsals in Busabala village

Our mission

Promoting culture through rhythm and performance. We bridge generations by teaching traditional drumming and dance, ensuring that Uganda's musical heritage stays alive and electrifying.

Our vision

Preserving and showcasing African drum traditions on global stages. We envision a world where the djembe, engalabi, and entamiiwu drums are recognised as messengers of unity and pride.

Master drummer & cultural keeper

Lukeman Kyotoyinze

Founder & artistic director

Lukeman grew up surrounded by the drums of Busabala. At age seven, he played his first entamiiwu at a local wedding. By twenty, he had studied under elders from five different clans, learning sacred rhythms and their stories.

In 2012, he founded Rwambera Drumline with a mission: to give young drummers a path, and to share the depth of Ugandan culture with the world. Under his guidance, the group has performed at the Kampala City Festival, the Bayimba International Festival, and community events across East Africa.

Lukeman is also a master builder of drums, harvesting wood and skin with traditional rituals. He believes "a drum is not just an instrument—it is the voice of the ancestors."

Rhythm lives in everyone. We just help you feel it.

Cultural impact

Youth mentorship

Over 150 young drummers trained since 2012, keeping them engaged and proud of their heritage.

Community celebrations

Rwambera is the heartbeat of Busabala's weddings, introductions, and harvest festivals.

Cultural exchange

Workshops with international schools and artists, spreading Ugandan rhythms worldwide.

Preserving traditions

Reviving nearly-lost rhythms like "Nankasa" and "Mbaga" for new generations.
